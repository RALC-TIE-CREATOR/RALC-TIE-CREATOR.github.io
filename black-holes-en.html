Why √M and not M?
In GR the horizon scales linearly with mass because c² is the only available scale constant: rₛ = 2GM/c². TIE adds a₀ as a second scale constant — and when you combine G, M and a₀ to build a length, the only dimensionally correct combination is \(\sqrt{GM/a_0}\). The square root is not a hypothesis — it is the only dimensionally valid way to construct a length from those three quantities.

What is the singularity-free interior?
In GR density diverges at r=0 — the singularity is a mathematical breakdown of the theory. In TIE, when the density of ħ-packets exceeds the infrastructure's capacity, the infrastructure saturates — packets become trapped in closed phase cycles. There is no physical r=0: the interior is a saturation region with a finite maximum density. The infrastructure does not break — it fills up.
