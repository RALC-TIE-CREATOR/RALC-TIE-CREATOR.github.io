In TIE, ħ is the synchrony quantum: the minimum infrastructure packet that can be processed in one cycle. A clock, to mark one second, must complete exactly N of these packets. In free space the infrastructure is homogeneous and processing is maximum. Near a mass the sync density rises: each packet costs more resistance, the clock completes fewer cycles per Absolute Motor pulse, and measured time appears slower — even though the Motor keeps the same pace.

The illusion of curved time
GR interprets this as spacetime curvature — an extremely effective mathematical tool. TIE proposes something simpler: time does not curve, clocks slow down. That difference has consequences at the extremes: TIE eliminates singularities because sync density has a finite physical maximum. GR predicts infinite densities at r=0. Current numbers cannot distinguish between them — future black hole observations will.
