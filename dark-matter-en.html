Why 2π and not another number?
Because \(a_0 = cH_0 / 2\pi\) — TIE's transition acceleration divides the universe's expansion rate by 2π. That same 2π appears in the mass because the infrastructure that generates gravity operates in complete cycles: one cycle = 2π radians. The visible fraction \(1/2\pi \approx 15.9\%\) is not a parameter fitted to data — it is a direct consequence of the cycle's geometry.

Why doesn't the bar change with mass?
Because \(1/2\pi\) is a pure mathematical constant, like π or e. It doesn't matter whether you measure the Sun, the Milky Way or the observable universe — always 84.1% of the real mass falls outside the Newtonian inventory. "Dark matter" does not vary from galaxy to galaxy: it is always exactly \(1 - 1/2\pi\).
